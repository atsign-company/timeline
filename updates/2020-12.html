On the fundraising side, we are well into our Series A raise. We have meetings set up with VCs in January, but we are happy to include a few more angels. If you know of anyone who might be interested, please let me know.

Apps status - We are implementing four cool new features to the @persona app to make it more interactive and viral that we plan to release very quickly. These are:

  1. The ability to more easily add rich media content to your public profile page.
  2. A simple means to “follow” another @sign and include them on your @persona page.
  3. Private P2P notifications when someone you follow updates their @profile so you know to check it out!
  4. Choice of themes and improvements to the layout of the @persona web pages.

We have also released updated versions of the @persona and @mosphere apps to the App Stores that include a lot of bug fixes and improvements, most notably to the process of pairing apps with your @sign to make it clear and less error prone (still more to do).

Renascence is still working to make the important @buzz app into a great @pp prior to fully releasing it. We are excited about what they are building and look forward to seeing it in the market as soon as possible.

The @rrive location sharing app is now in development by GeekyAnts and looks really amazing!

Lots more apps are coming soon, including new versions of @mospherePro designed for commercial users, and one funky little app we call the “dude app”, so please stay tuned for announcements as they are released for testing — you will be the first to know!

Now for the Techies!

For developers - We started a Discord Server where developers can interact with each other which is proving to be very popular. If you are a Discord fan, please join in the conversation.

We are also busy keeping the open source GitHub repo up to date and are now publishing libraries and UI components to the Dart and Flutter package manager pub.dev to make it super easy for developers to incorporate and use our SDKs, libraries and widgets in their apps.

@operations - Following the outage we had due to CloudFlare last month we have moved all of our operational DNS to NS1.com. NS1 provides high resiliency and automatic failover if any individual server fails. NS1 is in production now for both our websites and all of the secondary servers.

One of the challenges of running individual @servers is that they all need cryptographic certificates. The certificate renewal is now fully automated thanks to ZeroSSL and the use of certbot from the EFF.

For a while now we have been working with SunstoneDefense as we wanted to make sure we could not be attacked by robots. Normally this is achieved with a “Captcha”, something that asks you to click the pictures with shopfronts or lamp posts. That is never fun, so instead while using our website you might get asked to slide a security card into a slot or put your @sign in a bag, which is a much more fun and easy way to prove you are not a robot!

@core protocol - We have published a contacts library, which means that any @sign contacts you enter on an @pp will automatically appear as contacts in your other @pps. We have also been working hard on non repudiation of data, and we have cryptographic proof that even public and unencrypted data was created by you and has not been tampered with. Expanding this can address a lot of the fake content and copyright issues on the Internet today!

Please reach out if you have any questions or ideas that you would like to share!
