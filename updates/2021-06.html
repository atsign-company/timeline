The @ Company Update - June 2021

Wow, where did June go? I hope this email finds you well! The @ Company turned 2 in June and I celebrated with a post on @magic.

Lead with Free

We decided to lead with free @signs and make it easy for people to get them directly in the @apps. This was implemented first in @buzz, and we have updated the at_onboarding_widget and published it to pub.dev so that other apps can start using it. Expect all the @apps to be updated soon.

Our registrar site is currently being updated to lead with free @signs as well.

We have also been working on our next iteration of our developer site (https://atsign.dev). After taking into account the feedback that we received from the community, we added plenty of new technical content to make it easy for the developers to find what they are looking for. We plan to launch the new site in a couple of weeks.

We had a really fun Demo Day on Friday. Our participants in Africa always have a bit of a connection issue, but their apps are brilliant! If you are not on the Demo Day list and want to be, please let me know. Here is a playlist of past Demo Days, including Friday’s. We will load all future recordings of Demo Day here.

Denise and Tinashe did an amazing job running those and gathering feedback and ideas for how @buzz can be used. When you can share information completely privately and safely, you share much more than you would online today!

We have a really exciting hackathon with a group of doctors in July called FHIR @hack (https://fhirathack.devpost.com/). Who better to lead the way to a Privacy-First Internet than the medical community? We have more than 120 registrants from all over the globe signed up for the event and a great panel of judges from the medical and tech community.

On to the techy stuff:

Dart Null Safety: We have been making solid progress releasing Null Safety compliant updates to our published packages. As of now, 9 of the 19 active packages have been released with Null Safety on pub.dev.

More options for installing dess: One of our interns has been working to ensure that the personal secondary server can be installed on just about any combination of hardware (x86 or Arm) running a growing list of Linux distributions and in different cloud platforms such as Google or Amazon. This enables developers to create their own secondary servers within their own development environment and run everything except the root server for @signs.

@Login and self-sovereign identity: We never liked the idea of collecting email addresses or phone numbers on our registrar site, but we had to start somewhere. While you can already login using an @sign, we still rely on an email or SMS one time password (OTP) verification. We are currently developing @Login:a zero-trust, passwordless mechanism that eliminates the need to store any data about our customers at all. This is called self-sovereign identity. The next step to deliver on this is to release the @Login app and implement @Login with our registrar site as the first test case. The @Login app will also let you control how we should contact you should the need arise. After we perfect the system, we will make it easy to integrate into any website in a similar manner to traditional SSO mechanisms. We have another company interested in using this functionality for IOT devices.

I wish you the very best!
